SAFETY MANAGEMENT SOFTWARE FOR SOCIAL MEDIA &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; Sec.&#xA0;509.121.&#xA0;&#xA0;DEFINITIONS. In this subchapter: &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (1) &#xA0; &#xA0; "Child" means an individual who is under 17 years &#xA0; of age. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (2) &#xA0; &#xA0; "Department" means the Department of Information &#xA0; Resources. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (3) &#xA0; &#xA0; "Large social media platform" means a social media &#xA0; platform to which Chapter 120 applies. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (4) &#xA0; &#xA0; "Third-party safety software provider" means an &#xA0; entity that provides software designed to manage online &#xA0; interactions, content, and account settings for the safety of &#xA0; children. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (5) &#xA0; &#xA0; "User data" means any information needed to have a &#xA0; profile on a large social media platform or content on a large &#xA0; social media platform, including images, video, audio, or text, &#xA0; that is created by or sent to a child on or through the child's &#xA0; account with the platform. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; Sec. &#xA0; 509.122. &#xA0; &#xA0; REQUIREMENTS FOR LARGE SOCIAL MEDIA &#xA0; PLATFORMS. (a) A large social media platform shall create, &#xA0; maintain, and make available to third-party safety software &#xA0; providers a set of real-time application programming interfaces &#xA0; that allow a child or a parent or legal guardian of a child to &#xA0; delegate permission to a third-party safety software provider to &#xA0; manage the online interactions, content, and account settings of &#xA0; the child on the large social media platform on the same terms as &#xA0; the child. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (b) &#xA0; &#xA0; The application programming interfaces must be designed &#xA0; to allow third-party safety software providers to effectively &#xA0; manage and monitor a child's online activities and provide &#xA0; protections against cyberbullying, human trafficking, illegal drug &#xA0; distribution, sexual harassment, and violence. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (c) &#xA0; &#xA0; A large social media platform shall establish and &#xA0; implement reasonable policies, practices, and procedures regarding &#xA0; the secure transfer of user data to third-party safety software &#xA0; providers. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (d) &#xA0; &#xA0; In the case of a delegation made by a child or a parent &#xA0; or legal guardian of a child under this section, the large social &#xA0; media platform shall disclose to the child and the parent or legal &#xA0; guardian of the child that the delegation has been made and provide &#xA0; a summary of the user data that has been transferred to the &#xA0; third-party safety software provider. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; Sec. &#xA0; 509.123. &#xA0; &#xA0; IMPLEMENTATION BY DEPARTMENT. The department &#xA0; shall: &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (1)&#xA0;&#xA0;oversee the implementation of this subchapter; &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (2) &#xA0; &#xA0; establish guidelines and standards for the &#xA0; application programming interfaces and ensure compliance by large &#xA0; social media platforms; &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (3) &#xA0; &#xA0; conduct regular audits and assessments to ensure &#xA0; that large social media platforms are in compliance with the &#xA0; requirements of this subchapter; and &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (4) &#xA0; &#xA0; provide resources and support to parents and legal &#xA0; guardians using third-party safety software services to effectuate &#xA0; the protection of children from dangers including cyberbullying, &#xA0; human trafficking, illegal drug distribution, sexual harassment, &#xA0; and violence on large social media platforms. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; Sec. &#xA0; 509.124. &#xA0; &#xA0; REPORTING REQUIREMENTS. (a) A large social &#xA0; media platform shall submit an annual report not later than January &#xA0; 1 to the department detailing the platform's compliance with the &#xA0; requirements of this subchapter. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (b) &#xA0; &#xA0; The department shall submit an annual summary of all &#xA0; reports submitted by large social media platforms under this &#xA0; section not later than February 1 to the governor, the lieutenant &#xA0; governor, the speaker of the house of representatives, and each &#xA0; standing committee of the legislature with primary jurisdiction &#xA0; over large social media platforms highlighting the effectiveness of &#xA0; this subchapter and any areas needing improvement. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; Sec.&#xA0;509.125.&#xA0;&#xA0;AUTHENTICATION. The department shall: &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (1) &#xA0; &#xA0; issue guidance to facilitate the ability of a &#xA0; third-party safety software provider to obtain user data or access &#xA0; in a manner that ensures that a request for user data or access on &#xA0; behalf of a child is a verifiable request; and &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (2) &#xA0; &#xA0; issue guidance for large social media platforms &#xA0; and third-party safety software providers regarding the &#xA0; maintenance of reasonable safety standards to protect user data. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; Sec. &#xA0; 509.126. &#xA0; &#xA0; LIMITATION OF LIABILITY. In any civil action &#xA0; other than an action brought by the attorney general under &#xA0; Subchapter D, a large social media platform provider may not be held &#xA0; liable for damages arising out of the transfer of user data to a &#xA0; third-party safety software provider if the large social media &#xA0; platform has in good faith complied with the requirements of this &#xA0; subchapter and the guidance issued by the department under this &#xA0; subchapter. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; Sec. &#xA0; 509.127. &#xA0; &#xA0; USER DATA DISCLOSURE. A third-party safety &#xA0; software provider may not disclose any user data obtained under &#xA0; this subchapter to another person except: &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (1) &#xA0; &#xA0; under a lawful request from a governmental body, &#xA0; including for law enforcement purposes or for judicial or &#xA0; administrative proceedings; &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (2) &#xA0; &#xA0; to the extent that the disclosure is required by &#xA0; law and the disclosure complies with and is limited to the relevant &#xA0; requirements of such law; &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (3) &#xA0; &#xA0; to the child or a parent or legal guardian of the &#xA0; child who made a delegation under this subchapter and whose data is &#xA0; at issue, with the third-party safety software provider making a &#xA0; good faith effort to ensure that the disclosure includes only the &#xA0; user data necessary for a reasonable parent or guardian to &#xA0; understand that the child is experiencing or is at foreseeable risk &#xA0; to experience harm; &#xA0; &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (4) &#xA0; &#xA0; in the case of a reasonably foreseeable serious &#xA0; and imminent threat to the health or safety of any individual, if &#xA0; the disclosure is made to a person or persons reasonably able to &#xA0; prevent or lessen the threat; and &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (5) &#xA0; &#xA0; to a public health authority or other appropriate &#xA0; government authority authorized by law to receive reports of child &#xA0; abuse or neglect. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; Sec. &#xA0; 509.128. &#xA0; &#xA0; DISCLOSURE REPORTING. A third-party safety &#xA0; software provider that makes a disclosure permitted by this &#xA0; subchapter shall promptly inform the child with respect to whose &#xA0; account the delegation was made and the parent or legal guardian &#xA0; that a disclosure has been or will be made, unless: &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (1) &#xA0; &#xA0; the third-party safety software provider, in the &#xA0; exercise of professional judgment, believes informing the child or &#xA0; parent or legal guardian would place the child at risk of serious &#xA0; harm; or &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; (2) &#xA0; &#xA0; the third-party safety software provider is &#xA0; prohibited by law from informing the child or parent or legal &#xA0; guardian. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0; Sec. &#xA0; 509.129. &#xA0; &#xA0; CONFLICT WITH OTHER LAW. To the extent of any &#xA0; conflict between this subchapter and another provision of this &#xA0; chapter, this subchapter controls. &#xA0; &#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;&#xA0;SECTION&#xA0;3.&#xA0;&#xA0;This Act takes effect September 1, 2025.
